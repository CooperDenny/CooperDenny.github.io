Project Overview

This project dives into predicting the AFL Brownlow Medal votes, using data compiled from various sources such as the `fitzRoy` R package, afl.com.au, AFL Tables, and the AFL Coaches Association. By incorporating a range of offensive and defensive statistics, team metrics, AFL Coaches Association votes, and variables regarding past recognition such as previous Brownlow performances, my model aims to capture the key aspects of player performance, allowing for a nuanced understanding of what contributes to recognition from the umpires.

2023 Results:

The analysis of the predictions compared to the actual votes for the Brownlow Medal revealed several important insights into the model’s performance:

  • The model did not correctly predict the eventual winner, Lachie Neale, but in terms of error metrics, the difference between the model’s predicted votes and the actual votes for each player was quite accurate.
  • When taking a subset of players predicted to poll votes and/or who actually polled votes (removing players predicted to poll 0 votes and actually polled 0 votes), the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) was 2.165, indicating that, on average, the predicted votes differed from the actual votes by about 2.165 votes.
  • The model also correctly predicted the player to poll 3 votes in 56.52% of matches (117 out of 207).

I am using the same model to predict votes for the 2024 season.
